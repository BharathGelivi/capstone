Under what circumstances does the right of private defence of the body NOT extend to voluntarily causing the assailant's death?

The right of private defence of the body does not extend to the voluntary causing of death to the assailant if the offence is not of any of the descriptions specified in section 38 [Chunk-ID: d3d93d06-b511-4735-9b84-6085daecad6e]. This means that the right of private defence of the body only extends to causing death in specific circumstances, such as when the offence may reasonably cause the apprehension of death or grievous hurt [Chunk-ID: d3d93d06-b511-4735-9b84-6085daecad6e]. In other cases, the right of private defence of the body extends to causing harm other than death, under the restrictions specified in section 37 [Chunk-ID: d3d93d06-b511-4735-9b84-6085daecad6e].
